Overview

TESDA's main goal is to provide Filipinos with skills and knowledge that are relevant to various industries, thus enhancing their employability and contributing to the country's economic development.

Here is an overview of TESDA's functions, programs, and initiatives:

Skills Development Programs:

TESDA offers a wide range of technical vocational education and training (TVET) programs. These programs are designed to equip individuals with practical skills that are aligned with the demands of industries such as automotive, construction, healthcare, information technology, hospitality, agriculture, and more.

Training and Certification:

TESDA provides training and assessment services, leading to the issuance of National Certificates (NC) and Certificates of Competency (COC). These certificates serve as formal recognition of an individual's skills and competencies, making it easier for them to find employment or start their own businesses.

TVET Institutions:

TESDA oversees a network of technical vocational institutions, including TESDA Technology Institutions (TTIs), TESDA Technology Centers (TTCs), and TESDA-Accredited Training Centers (TATCs). These institutions offer various training programs to help learners develop their skills.

TVET Trainers:

TESDA also focuses on the development of TVET trainers. It provides training for trainers to ensure that they are equipped with the latest teaching methods and industry-specific knowledge.

Enterprise-Based Training:

TESDA collaborates with industries and employers to offer enterprise-based training programs. This approach helps ensure that the training provided is directly relevant to the needs of the labor market.

Special Programs:

TESDA initiates special programs for specific target groups, such as overseas Filipino workers (OFWs), indigenous peoples, persons with disabilities, and out-of-school youth. These programs aim to provide opportunities for marginalized groups to acquire skills and improve their livelihoods.

Online Learning:

TESDA has also embraced online platforms to provide accessible training materials and resources to learners. This includes the TESDA Online Program (TOP), which offers free online courses in various skill areas.

Partnerships:

TESDA collaborates with government agencies, private sector organizations, and international institutions to enhance the quality of TVET and ensure the alignment of skills with industry needs.
